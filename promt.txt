This is a strong foundation for a noir-style detective story. I've expanded it into a more complete game narrative while keeping your main ideas intact.

Detective Ska
Story Overview
Detective Ska was once one of the most respected investigators in the city. He lived a quiet life with his wife, Elena, the only person who ever managed to bring light into his dark world.

One rainy evening, Elena vanished without a trace.

The police found no evidence. No witnesses. No suspects.

Weeks passed.

Unable to cope with the loss, Ska spent most nights alone in bars, drowning himself in beer and regret. He blamed himself for not protecting her.

One night, while sitting in his usual corner at a rundown bar, he overheard a conversation between the bartender and a stranger. The stranger mentioned a woman connected to several unsolved disappearances over the past decade.

The description sounded disturbingly familiar.

The bartender recognized Ska's interest and reluctantly revealed a rumor:

A mysterious woman had appeared throughout the years in multiple cities. Wherever she went, men disappeared or were found dead under strange circumstances.

When Ska demanded more information, the bartender refused to continue.

(Here the player may enter a bar fight sequence.)

After the incident, Ska begins his own investigation.

Investigation
The city contains three abandoned apartment buildings.

Each building contains several floors and rooms.

Inside the rooms, the player finds clues:

Old newspaper articles.

Photographs with faces scratched out.

Wedding certificates.

Handwritten diaries.

Police reports.

Missing person files.

Audio recordings.

Letters signed only with the initials "E."

At first, the clues seem unrelated.

But a pattern slowly emerges.

Every newspaper article tells a similar story:

A man disappears.

A husband dies mysteriously.

A woman is questioned but never charged.

The names change.

The cities change.

But the photographs always contain the same woman.

Elena.

As Ska continues searching, he discovers that his wife has been married many times before.

Each husband eventually died.

Each case was ruled an accident, suicide, or disappearance.

Yet the evidence found throughout the buildings suggests something much darker.

The deeper Ska digs, the more he realizes that he may never have truly known the woman he loved.

Final Building
The third building is the oldest.

The top floor contains a single locked room.

After collecting every major clue, the player gains access.

The room is completely dark.

Dust covers everything.

A lone desk stands in the center.

On the desk rests an old cassette recorder.

Beside it lies a note:

"To my final husband."

The player presses PLAY.
